## Overview

This task involved researching and documenting social engineering attack types — focusing on phishing, pretexting, and baiting — including real-world case studies and prevention recommendations. The report covers how these attacks work, why they are so effective, and how organisations can protect themselves.

The goal was to demonstrate the ability to research complex security topics, synthesise information from credible sources, and present findings in a structured, professional format suitable for a technical or management audience.

## Report Structure

1. **Introduction** – Definition of social engineering and why it is considered one of the most effective attack vectors.
2. **Phishing** – Types (spear phishing, whaling, vishing, smishing), how it works, a real-world case study, and 4 prevention recommendations.
3. **Pretexting** – Definition, how an attacker builds a false scenario, a documented case study, and 3 prevention measures.
4. **Baiting** – Physical and digital baiting (infected USB drives, fake downloads), a case study, and 3 prevention measures.
5. **Quid Pro Quo** – Explanation and prevention (bonus).
6. **Comparison Table** – Attack type, primary target, psychological lever exploited, and best countermeasure.
7. **Organisational Recommendations** – A 5-point employee security awareness training checklist.
8. **Conclusion** – Key takeaways for organisations.
9. **References** – 4+ credible sources.
